Computational Neuroscience

At Stanford, we use a random white noise sequence to visually stimulate retinal ganglion cells and evoke a response. The spike triggered average (top-left) is calculated from spikes (bottom-left) with our custom lab software. A mosaic of receptive fields (right) can then be calculated, for multiple cell types. We then proceed to electrically stimulate cells to mimick the spikes produced by the visual stimulus. Essentially, we decode the signals and try to encode them by electrical stimulus.
